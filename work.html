Web Development

On this website we highlight some of the most recent projects we have undertaken. Please contact us for more examples or to discuss details.

Mobile First Design and Development

Research shows that an increasing percentage of web traffic happens on mobile devices. To ensure consistent branding for your business, we employ the practice of centering our design and construction around mobile device optimization. This ensures that your users get an ideal browsing experience, no matter what device they use to access your site.




Examples of Work:

April 2014 - Taylor Made Homes

Taylor Made Homes had a small but functional website built and hosted with Wix. In April of 2014 they asked us to help them give their business a fresh, clean look.

Goals for their new website:
  • Responsive design
  • Emphasize their lot for sale
  • Give prospective clients access to photos of past projects
  • Be highly searchable
Tablet and Desktop Displays

On devices with larger displays we endeavor to maintain a look and feel that is consistent with the mobile version but with functionality that takes advantage of the increase in visual real estate. Some changes in this case included:

  • Larger, higher resolution photos
  • More photos loaded in each photo gallery
  • Expanded menu

Visit live site at www.joetaylormadehomes.com

March 2014 - Eugene Myofascial Release

Like TMH, Eugene Myofascial Release had gotten by on an owner-made, single page, static website for many years. As their business grew, EMFR wanted to increase their web presence. They hired us to help them achieve that goal.

Their focus was on:
  • Clean, simple design
  • A website that felt calming, and comfortable, just like their healing work
  • Improving the form and function on mobile devices
  • Maintaining their high SEO index
  • Conserving the core style of their existing site
Technical Highlights

To achieve simplicity, this website was written by hand. Responsiveness was gained by employing media queries to control the layout on various devices. A simple jQuery plugin was employed to include a carousel of images of EMFR's studio and the practice of myofascial release.

Visit live site at www.eugenemyofascialrelease.com
